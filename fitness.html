Fitness

Strengthen your game from the ground up with agility, endurance, and focus.

Tennis Fitness

Train your body to match your game. Strength, agility, and endurance power every shot and recovery.

Coach’s Tip: Before every block, say: “Feet first, breathe steady, finish tall.” Your legs buy your swing time.

Weekly Goal (in-season): 2× movement blocks (15–20 min), 2× strength circuits (20 min), 2× mobility finishes (8–10 min).


Ladder footwork drills tailored for tennis — sharpen first-step quickness, balance, and spatial awareness.

Movement & Agility

Explosive first steps and smooth recoveries win rallies before the swing begins.

  • Split-Step Repeats: 4×20s on / 20s off. React to a visual cue; land the split as a partner “hits.” Cue: Split at their contact.
  • Lateral Shuffle Ladder: 5×(right-left-right-left), touch outside cone, stay low. Rest 30s. Goal: Quiet upper body.
  • Recover Races: Alley → hash → baseline, return to center; 6 reps. Focus: Last step is forward, not sideways.
  • 4-Ball Pattern: Wide FH → back to middle → wide BH → short in; shadow or feed. 3 rounds.

If you feel rushed, add height on the next ball. If you’re set, take on the rise.

Strength & Stability (20-Minute Circuit)

Balanced strength keeps you powerful and resilient through long matches. Minimal equipment: bands, light dumbbells, med ball.

  • Split-Squat (DB or bodyweight): 3×8/leg — slow down, drive up. Cue: Knee tracks over toes.
  • Hip Hinge (RDL or band good-morning): 3×10 — feel hamstrings; neutral spine. Cue: Hips back, shins vertical.
  • Anti-Rotation Press (Pallof): 3×10/side — resist twist; exhale as you press.
  • Scap Push-up or Band Row: 3×12 — shoulder blades glide; neck long.
  • Single-Leg RDL Reach: 2×8/leg — balance + hinge; tap toe to reset if needed.

Rest ~45s between exercises. Rotate continuously; quality > load.

Endurance & Conditioning

Sustain your best tennis for every point—train rhythm, recovery, and breath control.

  • 15/15 Baseline Intervals: 10 rounds of 15s corner-to-corner shuffle + 15s rest. Goal: Same speed in rounds 1 and 10.
  • Serve-Recovery Runs: 10× each side — serve → recover to hash → split → shadow +1. Walk back between reps.
  • Ghost Game (6 min): Coach calls “deep middle / angle / approach”; players move and shadow the pattern on cue.

Pair these with rally games on the Drills page.

Flexibility & Recovery

Protect joints and enhance movement efficiency through consistent mobility work.

  • Dynamic Warm-up (5–7 min): Leg swings, arm circles, walking lunges, karaoke, A-skips.
  • Cooldown Stretch (5 min): Hamstrings, hip flexors, calves, glutes, shoulders. Hold 20–30s each.
  • T-Spine + Hips (3 min): Open-book rotations ×6/side; 90/90 hip switches ×10.

Consistency beats intensity—finish every practice with mobility.

Mental & Team Fitness

Confidence grows when body and mind move together. Train focus, calm, and connection.

  • Box Breathing (2 min): In 4 — hold 2 — out 6 — hold 2. Use between games.
  • 3-Point Visualization: Picture serve +1, return pattern, and a pressure point you close at net.
  • Relay Finisher: Teams of 3–4: shuffle → sprint → backpedal → touch and tag. 3 races.

Readiness Checklist

  • ⬜ Explosive first step in every drill
  • ⬜ Balanced recovery to the hash mark
  • ⬜ Completed cooldown stretch + 1 mobility focus
  • ⬜ Hydration + simple post-practice snack packed
  • ⬜ Logged today’s numbers and next cue

Weekly Challenge: Hit all three pillars: 2 movement blocks, 2 strength circuits, and 2 mobility finishes. Share one clip + one takeaway in team chat.

In-Season 2-Day Template

Day A — Movement + Strength

  • Movement & Agility block (15–20 min)
  • Strength circuit (20 min)
  • Mobility finish (8–10 min)

Day B — Conditioning + Court

  • Endurance block (15–20 min)
  • Drills: serve +1 / return deep middle (20–30 min)
  • Cooldown + breathing (6–8 min)

Adjust volume during match weeks; quality reps beat fatigue.
