Overhead

Own the high ball. Read early, move your feet, and finish above the contact with calm hands.

Overhead Fundamentals

  • Read Early: Track the lob off the strings; turn shoulders immediately.
  • Side-On: Unit turn with the racquet up; non-dominant hand points to the ball.
  • Contact High: Strike fully extended, slightly in front; finish through your target.

Simple rule: turn, track, and take it high.

Footwork & Positioning

  • Crossover Steps: Turn and shuffle or crossover back sideways to stay aligned.
  • Behind the Bounce: Beat the ball to the spot; set under it, not backpedaling late.
  • Recover Forward: After contact, step through toward the net and reset split.

Contact & Cueing

  • Keep the tossing/non-dominant hand up to help spacing, then pull down to trigger rotation.
  • Compact, over-the-head swing path; avoid wind-up. Think "serve in the air."
  • Pick a big target: deep middle or into open court.

Overhead Types

Standard Overhead

Taken high and in front at mid-net or closer. Step through and finish to deep middle or angle away.

Scissor-Kick Overhead

When moving back: jump with a rear-to-front scissor action to transfer weight forward through contact.

Bounce (Let-It-Bounce) Overhead

If late or wind-affected, let it bounce, get behind it, then strike like a high forehand/serve hybrid.

Common Errors

  • Backpedaling late under the ball.
  • Huge backswing that ruins timing.
  • Hitting down too steeply and clipping the tape.

Fix it: turn early, compact swing, and big targets.
