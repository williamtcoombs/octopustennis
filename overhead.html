Overhead

Finish points with balance, aggression, and poise under pressure.

Overhead

Own the high ball. Read early, move your feet, and finish above contact with calm hands. Your overhead should feel like a serve in the air.

Coach’s Cue: “Turn → Track → Up.” Eyes steady, compact swing, big target.

Video Coaching Insight

Track with the off‑hand, turn early, and contact high with a simple, serve‑like motion.

Coach’s Tip: Watch in 0.75× for footwork rhythm; drop to 0.5× to check contact height and follow‑through.

Overhead Fundamentals

  • Read Early: Track the lob off the strings and turn the shoulders immediately.
  • Side‑On: Racquet up; non‑dominant hand points to the ball to keep spacing.
  • Contact High: Strike fully extended, slightly in front; finish through your target.

Simple rule: turn, track, and take it high.

Team Standard: Convert 8/10 sitter overheads to deep middle or clean angle without clipping the tape.

Footwork & Positioning

  • Crossover Steps: Shuffle or crossover back sideways to stay aligned — avoid straight backpedal.
  • Behind the Bounce: Beat the ball to the spot; set under it, not drifting late.
  • Recover Forward: After contact, step through toward the net and split for the next ball.

Cue: “Feet first.” Your legs buy the swing time.

Contact & Cueing

  • Keep the off‑hand up until the last moment; pull it down to trigger rotation.
  • Compact, over‑the‑head path — no wind‑up. Think “serve in the air.”
  • Pick a big target: deep middle or into open court.

Reset Cue: Exhale as you load under the ball → say “Up” at contact → hold your finish for a one‑count.

Overhead Types

Standard Overhead

Taken high and in front at mid‑net or closer. Step through and finish to deep middle or angle away.

Goal: 8/10 to deep middle with height over the net‑strap.

Scissor‑Kick Overhead

Moving back? Jump with a rear‑to‑front scissor action to transfer weight forward through contact.

Goal: Land moving forward after contact 7/10 reps.

Bounce (Let‑It‑Bounce) Overhead

If late or wind‑affected, let it bounce, get behind it, then strike like a high forehand/serve hybrid.

Goal: 6/8 clean put‑aways after a controlled bounce.

Defensive Overhead

Under pressure? Play deep middle with height to reset; don’t force the winner.

Goal: 7/10 successful resets that keep you in offense after the next ball.

Swinging Volley Overhead

Ball high and sitting? Use a compact forehand swing above shoulder height; drive to open court.

Goal: 6/8 finished to space with balanced recovery.

Backpedal Overhead (Emergency)

When you can’t turn in time: short steps back, eyes steady, shorten swing, play to big margin.

Goal: No more than 1 miss into the tape per set from this pattern.

Patterns & Decisions

  • Deep Middle First: Highest margin on first overhead; go for the sharp angle once you’re balanced.
  • Drop → Lob Read: After a short ball, expect another lob — be ready to turn instantly.
  • Follow Forward: Overhead to deep middle → close the net and split for the next ball.

Goal: On a two‑ball pattern, win 7/10 points when the first overhead goes deep middle.

Drills

  • Turn & Track Series: Coach lobs alternating sides; player turns, points with off‑hand, finishes high.
  • Scissor‑Kick Ladder: Progressive deeper lobs — execute scissor and land moving forward.
  • Bounce Overhead: Let it bounce by design; step behind and strike through target.
  • Deep Middle First: First overhead to deep center, second to angle — repeat.

Blend these with approach and net patterns on the Volley page.

Weekly Challenge: In practice sets, miss ≤1 overhead per set and win 70% of rallies where you touch an overhead.

Common Errors

  • Backpedaling late under the ball.
  • Huge backswing that ruins timing.
  • Hitting down too steeply and clipping the tape.

Fix: Film 10 overheads. Grade: early turn, spacing with off‑hand, contact height, follow‑through direction.

Overhead Checklist

  • ⬜ Immediate shoulder turn
  • ⬜ Point with off‑hand; contact high and in front
  • ⬜ First target deep middle
  • ⬜ Recover forward and split
