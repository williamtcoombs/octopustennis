Strategy

Make smart choices under pressure: simple priorities, trusted patterns, and a clear plan between points.

Match Strategy

Strategy is the bridge between your skills and the score. When in doubt: play deep to the middle, change direction only on balanced balls, and attack short balls with margin.

Coach’s Tip: Say this at the baseline: “Height. Depth. Middle.” Your targets create your confidence.

Between-Point Routine (BPR)

  1. Reset: Turn away, exhale, towel if needed. Let the last point go.
  2. Read: Score + opponent pattern + wind/sun. What’s the threat?
  3. Plan: One sentence: “Serve T → +1 middle.” / “High CC → attack short.”
  4. Cue: A single word at the line: Height, Middle, or Feet.

Coach’s Tip: Use strings/laces as a trigger: touch, breathe, commit.

Game Plans vs. Opponent Types

Grinder / Retriever

  • Height + depth to middle; take time on your terms.
  • Attack short balls with approach to body; finish at net.
  • Key cue: Win the middle, then the point.

Big Hitter

  • Neutralize with deep cross + body targets.
  • Make them hit one more ball at chest height.
  • Key cue: Height over net buys time.

Net Rusher

  • Low to feet first; mix topspin lob over backhand shoulder.
  • Serve body / T to jam first volley.
  • Key cue: Feet before pass/lob.

Counterpuncher / “Pusher”

  • Don’t over-hit. Build with height and patient depth.
  • Use drop → lob pattern when they camp deep.
  • Key cue: Middle until they blink.

Scoreboard Priorities

  • Up 30–0: Higher margin; body/hip targets; build pressure.
  • 30–30 / Deuce: Middle first. Earn your angle on ball two.
  • Break point (returning): Make first strike cross-court and deep.
  • Set point: Use your bread-and-butter pattern; no experiments.

Weekly Challenge: Track 20 big points; aim for 70% execution of your planned pattern.

Environment Adjustments

  • Into wind: Add drive; bigger targets; step in on short balls.
  • With wind: Add height/spin; play more through middle.
  • Sun high: Favor slice and drop to keep bounce low; adjust overhead footwork early.

Conditions change plans—your principles (height, depth, middle) don’t.
