Strategy

The Octopus framework for building intelligent positioning, pressure, and patterns — so your tennis scales under stress.

Strategy is the why behind the where. While tactics choose shots, strategy shapes the court, tempo, and options you leave yourself.

1. Win the Map Before the Rally

Your first goal is to control space: start neutral, create advantage, exploit it, then reset. Think in phases and invest in positioning that compounds later.

2. Build Sustainable Pressure

Consistency, depth, and height are pressure. Use margin to make your patterns repeatable under stress. Don’t just hit harder — remove air from your opponent’s options.

3. Think in Patterns, Not Points

Winning rallies comes from chains, not single strikes. Define your A/B patterns from common ball feeds and rehearse them until they run on rails.

4. Adapt Like an Octopus

Change shape as the situation changes. If they rush the net, thread through lanes; if they camp deep, take time away; if they moonball, step in and reframe height vs. depth.

5. Train Decision, Not Just Execution

Skill under pressure is decision + action. Add time constraints, variable feeds, and score pressure to practice so your choices become automatic and robust.

6. From Strategy to Tactics

Once the why is clear, the what gets easier. Pick patterns that express your strengths, disrupt theirs, and scale as matches get tight.

“Fluid, connected, and intelligent — like the octopus itself.”
