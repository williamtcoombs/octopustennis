Strategy

Play smart: big margins, simple choices, relentless consistency — then strike when you’ve earned it.

Strategy

Strategy is your match-wide plan. It decides how you want points to feel before you worry about individual patterns.

It should stay simple enough that you can repeat it out loud on a changeover and remember it when you’re tired.

Coach’s Cue: “Plan → Breathe → Commit.” If you don’t have a plan, default to a high, deep ball to the middle and reset.

Core Pillars

These are your non-negotiables. If a point goes badly, check which pillar you ignored.

  • Percentages: Aim for big targets, high net clearance, and spin. If you miss, miss long — not in the tape.
  • Pressure: Build height and depth first; look to finish after you’ve moved the opponent out of position.
  • Patience: Stay in your patterns. Win “boring” points by making them hit one more quality ball.
  • Position: Recover inside the hash. Own the middle until you’ve clearly earned the angle.

Standard: In a match, 65–70% of points should start with a high-margin target (deep middle or body).

Between-Point Routine (BPR)

Use a short, repeatable routine to connect your strategy to every single point.

  1. Plan: Name your target and purpose out loud (build / pressure / finish).
  2. Breathe: 4-count inhale, 6-count exhale; relax jaw, hands, and shoulders.
  3. Commit: Use a one-word cue at bounce (“Height” / “Middle” / “Up”) and trust it.

If you feel doubt or panic, go back to “deep middle with height” and rebuild from there.

Scoreboard Rules

The score should change your margin, not your identity. Use these simple defaults:

  • Up 30–0 / 40–15: Add pressure with depth; look for angles on the second ball, not the first.
  • 30–30 / Deuce: Highest-margin targets only — deep middle or body.
  • Break Points (for or against): Serve or return to the big target you trust most and play first ball with margin.
  • Tie-breakers: Prioritize first-ball height; no off-balance “hero” swings.

Target: Hold serve 75%+ by playing first-ball to margin.

Environment & Adjustments

Conditions don’t change who you are as a player, but they do change the safest way to express your game. Use simple rules you can remember on the baseline.

  • Wind:
    Into the wind – Think “drive and brave.” Hit a bit more through the ball with solid racquet speed, aim a little deeper than usual, and still use spin. The wind is your brake, so you can trust a firmer swing to a big target without floating the ball.
    With the wind – Think “high and heavy, land it shorter.” Add topspin, not just height, and aim 2–3 feet shorter than your normal deep target. Let the wind carry the ball deeper for you. The cue is: “Brush more, don’t pop it up.”
  • Sun: When hitting into glare, avoid low or flat shots that require precise depth judgment. Favor cross-court because the net is lower and the court is longer, and use higher net clearance so you’re not aiming at small targets you can’t see well. If vision is really compromised, send a calm cross-court lob to buy time and reset your eyes.
  • Surface: Slow courts (like hard courts on a cold day) give your opponent more time, so extend rallies with big cross-court targets and trust your legs—patterns last longer, and patience wins. Fast courts (warm hard courts or slick indoor surfaces) shorten reaction time, so simplify your plan to first-strike tennis: a clear Serve+1 and Return+1, bigger targets, and early offense when the ball sits up. Let the court speed decide whether you play longer patterns or take initiative sooner.

Standard: After a side change, play the next game with zero tape errors by using your wind and surface adjustments on every first ball.

Strategic Game Plans

These are simple “big-picture” plans. Tactics on the next page are how you execute them point by point.

Serve-First Plan

Serve to big targets, play the first ball deep middle, then finish into open space.

Goal: Win 65–70% of Serve+1 exchanges.

See Tactics for Serve+1 trees.

Return-First Plan

Neutralize with height to the body or deep middle; step in on second serves.

Goal: Win about 45% of return games by forcing a short ball in the first three shots.

See Tactics for Return+1 branches.

Neutral to Offense

Rally cross-court until you see short / middle / height, then take space and pressure.

Cue: “Short or middle? Go.”

Defense to Reset

Raise height, aim deep middle, and recover inside the hash to buy time.

Goal: Reset to neutral within two balls at least 6/10 times.

Opponent Archetypes

Use simple labels so your team can agree quickly on what you’re facing, then choose a plan.

  • Big Hitter: Add height and depth; make them hit one more ball from uncomfortable positions.
  • Counter-puncher: Take balls early inside the court; finish to space, not to the line.
  • Net Rusher: Target body and feet first; lob or pass only when balanced.
  • All-Court: Win the middle and vary height and tempo to force them into making decisions.

Match Metrics & Review

Use simple numbers to check whether your strategy actually showed up in the match.

  • First-Ball Margin: 80%+ of first balls clear the net by 2–3 feet.
  • Serve+1 Win%: 65–70% target in practice sets.
  • Neutral Rally Wins: About 60% in rallies under 6 balls; 55% in rallies over 6 balls.
  • Emotional Errors: At most 1 rushed, low-margin swing per set.

Post-Match: Write down one principle you kept well and one you’ll emphasize next match.

Strategy Checklist

Use this as a quick reset between sets or before practice.

  • ⬜ First ball to a big target with height
  • ⬜ Recover inside the hash after every swing
  • ⬜ Decide the purpose before serve/return (build / pressure / finish)
  • ⬜ Run your BPR every point: Plan → Breathe → Commit

Weekly Challenge: In two practice sets, keep a simple BPR note on your towel and track Serve+1 win% and first-ball margin.

Coach’s Corner

Strategy

Focus Theme

Strategy is your program compass. Think in three layers — Principles → Plans → Plays — so every coach and player is speaking the same language on match day.

Layer 1 — Principles (Non-Negotiables)

  • Win with margin: height, depth, and spin first; “miss long, never in the tape.”
  • Neutral → Advantage → Finish: build pressure before going for the hero ball.
  • Own the middle: recover inside the hash until you’ve earned the angle.
  • One plan per point: choose build / pressure / finish in the BPR and stick to it.
  • Cool head, clear cue: players respond to simple cues (“Height”, “Middle”, “Up”) not speeches.

Layer 2 — Plans (Match & Set Plans)

  • Serve-First Plan: big targets on serve, first ball deep middle, finish to space. Pair this with Scoreboard Rules at 30–30, deuce, and break points.
  • Return-First Plan: neutralize to body or deep middle, step in on seconds, live cross-court until you earn short.
  • Neutral-to-Offense Plan: stay cross-court until you see short / middle / height, then attack.
  • Defense-to-Reset Plan: raise height, aim deep middle, recover inside the hash within two balls.
  • For each player, pick one primary plan to start the match and one backup to try after a side change.

Layer 3 — Plays (Point-Level Patterns)

  • “Two cross, one change”: build with two cross-court balls, change direction only when balanced.
  • “Deep middle reset”: under pressure, send a high, heavy ball deep middle and recover.
  • “Short or middle? Go.”: if they leave the ball short or middle, step in and pressure space.
  • “High then low”: lift deep with height, then pull them short (drop or short angle).
  • Use Tactics as the detailed playbook: Serve+1 trees, Return+1 branches, and pattern cards.

Coach Implementation Drill

In a team meeting, pick one upcoming match. As a staff (and captains), define: 2 core principles to emphasize, 1 primary plan per player (Serve-First, Return-First, or Neutral-to-Offense), and 2 go-to plays from the Tactics page. Write them on a simple card or in a notes app. Goal: after the match, each player can explain their plan and name at least one play they actually used under pressure.
