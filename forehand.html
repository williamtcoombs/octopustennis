Forehand

Build a forehand you trust under pressure: clean unit turn, contact in front, balanced finish, and big cross‑court margins.

Forehand

At Poolesville, your forehand is a field position weapon — depth first, shape second, pace third. We build a repeatable pattern: unit turn → space → contact in front → tall finish.

Pick big targets and let your legs load the shot. Power comes from posture and timing, not from forcing the swing.

Coach’s Cue: “Turn • Space • Front • Up.” Say it softly as the ball crosses the net.

Forehand Fundamentals

We want a reliable ball under pressure, not a highlight reel. Build the base and the pace will come.

  • Grip & Feel: Semi‑western or eastern is fine — keep the wrist soft and the string‑bed square at contact.
  • Unit Turn: Turn shoulders as you split; racquet up, elbow relaxed. Show your back to the target.
  • Spacing: Create room with the outside foot; keep comfortable hip‑to‑hand distance.
  • Contact in Front: Strike slightly ahead of the lead hip; eyes quiet at contact.
  • Finish Tall: Chest to target, palm to the sky feeling, balanced on the outside edge.

Consistency creates confidence; posture + legs create pace.

Team Standard: 8/10 cross‑court rally balls landing deep (past the service line) with height over the net strap.

Shapes & Targets

Choose a shape before the bounce. Margin first, damage second.

  • Cross‑Court (Default): Highest net, longest court. Use height and heavy spin.
  • Inside‑Out: Step around and send big to the backhand corner; recover through the middle.
  • Down‑the‑Line (Earned): Use when early and balanced; finish your weight into the court.
  • Body Middle: Neutralize pace; jam and reset when under pressure.

Goal: 6/10 balls land within a cross‑court cone (service line to corner) and bounce above hip height.

Footwork Patterns

Feet write the story. Match your move to the ball.

  • Step‑Through: On short/mid balls; drive through contact and recover forward.
  • Load‑Hop: On higher pace; load on outside leg and hop into balance after contact.
  • Open Stance: On deep/wide balls; turn early, rotate through, finish tall.
  • Emergency Block: Shorten swing, aim big middle, buy time.

Your first step decides your shape.

Pressure Plan

When the score tightens: breathe, choose the big lane, and raise your net clearance.

  • Default to cross‑court depth with height; take the line only when early.
  • Body‑middle to jam pace when rushed.
  • Call your shape before the bounce: “XC high,” “IO heavy,” or “Middle jam.”

Reset Cue: “Turn • Space • Front • Up.”

Forehand +1 Connection

Decide your follow‑up before you swing.

  • XC Heavy → Attack the open court to the backhand corner.
  • IO Big → Step through and look for the short ball to finish.
  • Middle Jam → Take time, then move the ball to space.

Drills & Practice

  • XC Cone Ladder: 20‑ball sets to a cross‑court cone; score balls that land past the service line.
  • IO Step‑Around: Feed to backhand corner → step around → inside‑out to deep deuce; 8/10 make‑rate.
  • Earn the Line: Rally XC; when coach calls “Early!” take the line and recover middle.

Common Errors & Quick Fixes

  • Late Contact: Ball flying long or wide → call “Front!” and move sooner; shorten the backswing.
  • Shanks: Crowding the ball → create spacing with an outside step before you swing.
  • Low Net Clips: Flat swing path → raise finish, use more net clearance.
  • Off‑Balance Finish: Over‑rotating → stick the landing tall; chest to target.

Self‑check: Film 8 forehands from the side; grade unit turn timing, contact in front, and finish balance.

  • ⬜ Contact clearly in front of lead hip

Coach’s Corner

Forehand

Focus Theme

Build a forehand that is reliable under pressure and dangerous when you choose to attack. The goal is simple: a repeatable swing shape that creates time, shape, and margin — not just power.

What to Reinforce

  • Unit turn first — coil early so the swing never feels rushed.
  • Stable base — wide stance, quiet head, smooth weight transfer.
  • Heavy, safe targets — crosscourt with shape; middle third when defending.

Common Red Flags

  • Arm-only swings with no hip or shoulder coil.
  • Contact drifting behind the body, especially under pressure.
  • Flattening out balls too early in the point instead of building shape.

Key Cues & Corrections

  • “Turn first, then swing.”
  • “Wide base, quiet head.”
  • “Shape the ball — height gives margin.”
  • If consistently late, start the unit turn as soon as you recognize the ball direction and reduce backswing by 20–30%.

Observation Drill

Record 15 forehands from rally pace. Track: timing of the unit turn, height over the net, and how often contact occurs in front of your hip. Target: 11 / 15 balls struck in front with 3–4 feet of net clearance.

Captain’s Checklist

Forehand

Captain’s Checklist — Forehand

Use this checklist to keep our forehand identity clear on every court: early unit turn, clean spacing, contact in front, and big cross-court margins. Captains keep the language simple, calm, and repeatable.

Before the Match

  • Confirm each player’s forehand identity (default shape and target on both sides).
  • Have players state their forehand goal for the match (e.g., “8/10 XC deep past the service line”).
  • Review the cue “Turn • Space • Front • Up” and when they’ll use it (rallies, pressure points).
  • For doubles, clarify who will hunt forehands in the middle and which patterns feature the forehand as the main weapon.

During the Match

  • At changeovers, ask: “Are you winning with depth and shape, or chasing speed?”
  • Watch for red flags: falling back, late contact, low net clearance, or over-swinging.
  • Remind players that cross-court heavy is the default — take the line only when early and balanced.
  • Have them rate forehand feel 1–5 and adjust: lower target and add height if confidence is low.

Tight Situations

  • Call the highest-confidence pattern — XC high and heavy, or middle jam to shrink angles.
  • Use calm cues: “Wide base, quiet head” and “Shape the ball.”
  • Raise net clearance and aim bigger targets; no low, flat hero swings on big points.
  • If errors pile up, go to reset pattern: cross-court rally ball with 3–4 feet of net clearance.

Between Sets

  • Have each player name one forehand pattern working and one needing adjustment.
  • Match feedback to cues: is the issue turn, spacing, contact in front, or finish?
  • Choose a simple plan for the next set: “XC heavy first, line only when early.”

Post-Match

  • Ask for one example point where their forehand pattern worked exactly as planned.
  • Ask for one point where the forehand broke down (late, cramped, or over-hit) and identify which cue was missing.
  • Assign one drill for next practice that directly matches the main issue (spacing, contact in front, or shape).

Captain’s Cue: “Turn early, play big cross-court, finish tall. Shape first, power second.”
