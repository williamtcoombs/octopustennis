Lob

Master the lob to reset the point, buy recovery time, and keep pressure on opponents who live at the net.

What the Lob Does

The lob buys time, resets when you’re pressured, and punishes opponents who camp the net. Your goals: get height, get depth, and recover. If they smash, you make them hit one more ball from an awkward spot; if they retreat, you take the court back.

Fundamentals

Setup & Footwork

  • Stance: Wide base, chest up, chin level. Think “lift with legs.”
  • Contact: Slightly in front, strings facing the sky; finish high over shoulder.
  • Spacing: Give yourself room; don’t get jammed. Use a drop step to create space.

Contact & Finish

  • Low → High: Brush up, not across. Let the racket “carry” the ball up.
  • Head still: Freeze after contact for one beat; watch the ball up the line it travels.
  • Relaxed grip: 3–4/10 grip pressure to feel the lift.

When to Lob

  • Defensive: You’re out of position or stretched wide → buy time and reset.
  • Offensive: Opponent crowds the net or cheats forward → punish the positioning and take the net after.
  • Disguise: Show a normal groundstroke setup, then lift late.

Targets & Height

  • Cross-court deep corner for margin, especially on the run.
  • Down-the-line if they lean middle or leave the sideline open.
  • Height: Minimum one and a half net heights in practice; two net heights when under pressure.

Common Mistakes

  • Too flat: Ball sits up for an easy overhead. Fix: exaggerate low-to-high and finish high.
  • Panic swing: Rushing contact. Fix: load the legs, slow your head, and lift.
  • Short middle: Aim deeper or more cross-court for margin.

Drills

  • Box height drill: Aim over a partner’s raised racket or a strap two net-heights above the net.
  • Corner targets: Cones in deep corners; must clear “two nets” to count.
  • Run & lift: Coach feeds wide; recover with a high lob and sprint back into the court.

Match Cues

  • Opponent camps at the net? Test their overhead early.
  • Wind behind you? Add more low-to-high and aim higher.
  • Wind in your face? Free margin — lift and let the wind help.
