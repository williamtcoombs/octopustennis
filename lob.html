Lob

Master the lob to reset the point, buy recovery time, and keep pressure on opponents who live at the net.

Lob

The lob is a high, deep, defensive or tactical shot that resets pressure, punishes crowding, and opens the court.

Key principles

  • Height first, depth second. Clear opponent +2 racket lengths; land past the service line toward baseline corners.
  • Use their position. Lob when opponent crowds the net or pinches center; punish poachers in doubles.
  • Shape over speed. Relax the wrist, use a longer, lofted finish; think parabola, not bullet.
  • Recover. After contact, turn and sprint to your best court position; don’t admire.

Technique checklist

  1. Set the base: Turn shoulders early, load on outside foot, racquet head below ball.
  2. Brush & lift: Contact slightly in front; low–to–high swing path with open racquet face 5–15°.
  3. Finish high: Palm to the sky; finish above head to add arc.
  4. Target window: Aim 15–20 ft over the net and deep cross to backhand corner.
  5. Recover: Split-step as the ball peaks; read opponent’s retreat or overhead prep.

When to use it

  • Opponent camps at the net or poaches aggressively (doubles).
  • You’re stretched wide and need a reset.
  • Opponent has weak overhead or slow first step back.

Avoid it when

  • Opponent has a strong overhead and is already retreating.
  • Ball is too low/deep to lift safely—choose a low slice or dipper instead.

Drills

1) Ladder Lobs (Consistency)

From baseline, hit 10 lobs aiming to land: (a) just past service line, (b) mid no-man’s land, (c) 3 ft from baseline. 3 rounds.

Goal: 24/30 in target zones.

2) Net Poacher Punish (Decision)

Coach feeds cross-court. If the net player pinches, auto-lob; if they hold, pass cross. Alternate every 3 balls.

Goal: 70% correct decisions; 60% successful outcomes.

3) Overhead Bait (Tactical)

Feed a shallower lob to provoke an overhead; recover and defend the overhead with a deep reset lob.

Goal: Win 6/10 points by turning defense into neutral.

Measurable goals

  • Practice: 8/10 lobs land deep (last 6 ft) with peak ≥ 15 ft over net.
  • Match: Convert 3 momentum swings per set using high, deep lobs.
  • Doubles: Force 4+ backpedals or awkward overheads per match.

Common mistakes

  • Flat swing—no arc, sits up for overhead.
  • Short landings—dies in service box.
  • Admiring the shot—late recovery.

Quick fixes

  • Exaggerate high finish; open face slightly.
  • Aim higher over net; choose deep cross to safer backhand side.
  • Say the cue: “Lob—recover!” and move immediately.

Match scenarios & cues

  • Vs. net rusher: First lob high/deep; next ball pass into the space they left.
  • Doubles I-formation: If server closes too tight, lob over the net player’s backhand shoulder.
  • Wind at your back: Reduce face angle and aim higher to counter carry.

Mental game

Treat the lob as a choice, not a bailout. Choose it early when the picture is right. Commit to height.

Cue words: “See height. Shape it. Recover.”

Coach’s notes

  • In practice, call out the intended peak height (“15 feet”) before each rep.
  • Track deep-land rate and decision accuracy in doubles.
  • Own the recovery step—your next shot is often the winner.
