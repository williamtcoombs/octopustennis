Lob

The lob is our tempo reset and spacing tool. Use height and depth to turn defense into neutral — and sometimes into offense. Keep decisions simple and early.

Coach’s note: Choose height on purpose, not as a bailout. Commit to the arc.

Key principles

  • Height first, depth second. Clear +2 racket lengths; land past the service line.
  • Use their position. Punish net rushers and poachers.
  • Shape over speed. Low–to–high, relaxed wrist, high finish.
  • Recover. Don’t admire — relocate and split.

Technique checklist

  1. Set: Early unit turn, outside-foot load, head under ball.
  2. Brush: Contact slightly in front; open face 5–15°.
  3. Lift: Finish above head — palm to sky.
  4. Window: Aim 15–20 ft over tape; deep corner bias.
  5. Recover: Read retreat vs. overhead; split on their prep.

Coach’s tips

  • Say it: “See height → Shape it → Recover.”
  • Pick a peak: Name a number (e.g., “15 feet”) before contact.
  • Safer side: Deep cross to backhand unless wide open.
  • No stare: Turn immediately after contact.

How To Win With A Great Lob

Lob Practice Drills for Tennis

When to use it

  • Net rusher crowds too close.
  • Poacher leaves backcourt open (doubles).
  • You’re stretched and need time to reset.

Avoid it when

  • Opponent has a strong overhead and is already retreating.
  • Contact is too low/deep for safe lift → choose a dipper/slice.

Drills & measurable goals

Consistency · Ladder Lobs

3 rounds × 10 balls — service line / mid-NML / baseline. Goal: 24 of 30 in zones.

Cue: “Height before depth.”

Decision · Poacher Punish

Auto-lob if the net player pinches; otherwise pass. Goal: 70% correct reads, 60% execution.

Cue: “Read early. Commit fully.”

Tactical · Overhead Bait

Provoke a shallower overhead, then defend with a deeper reset lob. Goal: Win 6 of 10 by flipping momentum.

Cue: “Reset high, win next.”

  • Practice target: 8 of 10 lobs land in the last 6 ft with peak ≥ 15 ft over tape.
  • Match target: Create 3 momentum shifts per set with high, deep lobs.
  • Doubles target: Force 4+ backpedals or awkward overheads per match.

Mindset & match application

  • Tactical patience: Lob to buy time, not to give up. Reset the rally and control tempo.
  • Wind discipline: Respect the breeze — aim higher, soften swing, and trust your margin.
  • Confidence cue: See height. Shape it. Recover. Don’t rush the rhythm.

Trust the arc, trust your movement — a confident lob wins the point twice: once in space, once in mindset.

Next skill: Overhead → turn your height advantage into confident finishes.

Every lob you trust teaches control under pressure — that’s how complete players are made, together.

Coach’s Corner — Lob

Focus Theme: Use height and depth on purpose. A good lob is a planned tempo reset or a punishment for crowding the net — not a panic shot. See the space, commit to the arc, and recover.

What to Reinforce

  • Height before depth — clear the ball by at least two racquet lengths; trust shape to bring it down.
  • Early read on positioning — if they’re tight to the net or poaching, lob is on before you swing.
  • Simple, smooth lift — low-to-high swing, relaxed wrist, no last-second “flicks.”
  • Clear peak target — pick an imaginary window 15–20 feet above the net, then swing to that window.
  • Immediate recovery — turn and move as soon as you feel contact; don’t admire the ball.

Common Red Flags

  • Lob chosen late — decision made after you’ve already started a drive swing.
  • Contact behind the body — reaching or falling away instead of lifting from under the ball.
  • Arc too low — “medium-height” balls that sit up perfectly for an overhead.
  • Zero recovery — staying in place to watch instead of sprinting to a better defensive spot.
  • Wind ignored — same swing in a strong breeze, leading to short or long misses in clusters.

Key Cues & Corrections

  • “See height → Shape it → Recover.”
  • “Higher than you think.” (In wind or vs. tall players.)
  • “Lift, don’t flick.”
  • “Reset high, win next.”
  • If you’re leaving lobs short, slow the swing, open the face slightly more, and aim for a higher window for 10 balls before tightening targets.

Observation Drill

Run a 20-ball lob set (fed or live) with a net player starting close to the tape. Track: height over net, landing zone (inside / outside last 6 ft), and whether the opponent hits a comfortable overhead. Targets: at least 16 / 20 in, with 12+ landing in the last 6 feet of the court and 14+ forcing the opponent to retreat or hit a difficult overhead. Note patterns (short, long, low) and choose one cue to emphasize in your next match.
