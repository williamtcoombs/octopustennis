Coach’s Corner — Program Framework

For coaches, captains, and curious players: Octopus Tennis is built as a coaching ecosystem, not just a list of tips. Every page fits into a simple loop we use at Poolesville: Strategy → Tactics → Drills → Fitness → Competition → Reflection.

How the Pages Fit Together

  • Strategy: Our big-picture Principles → Plans → Plays — how we want to win points over a whole match.
  • Tactics: The Tactics Compass — Tempo, Space, Identity, Pressure — for in-match adjustments when things get messy.
  • Drills: Where Strategy and Tactics become reps and games with clear scoring and targets.
  • Fitness: The engine that lets players hold their technique and make good decisions deep into matches.
  • Competition: How we prepare before the match, steer during it, and learn from it afterward.
  • Stroke & skills pages (Serve, Return, Forehand, etc.): Technical building blocks — each with a Coach’s Corner at the end to anchor teaching points.

For Coaches & Captains

  • Use Strategy and Tactics to choose a weekly theme (for example: “Middle first” or “Slow tempo, then strike”).
  • Build practices from the Drills page so every session clearly supports that theme.
  • Check Fitness to match conditioning to how we want to play: grinders, attackers, net-rushers, doubles specialists.
  • On match day, use Competition as the guide for pre-match meetings, changeover conversations, and post-match reflection.
  • When teaching a stroke, scroll to the Coach’s Corner at the end of that page for key cues, red flags, and observation drills.

For Players

  • Curious what your coach is thinking? Start with Strategy and Tactics. That’s the language behind most things you hear on court.
  • When a shot is giving you trouble, visit that stroke’s page, scroll to the Coach’s Corner at the bottom, and look at the “Key Cues” and “Observation Drill.”
  • Use the Drills page to find one or two drills you can repeat on your own or with a teammate.
  • After matches, use Competition to reflect: Which plans worked? Which patterns showed up? What do you want to train next week?

The big idea: this site is here so our whole program shares one clear playbook — coaches, captains, and players all pulling in the same direction.
