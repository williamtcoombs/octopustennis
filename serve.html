Serve

Start every point with purpose. A strong serve isn’t about power — it’s about control, rhythm, and confidence.


Serve Fundamentals

  • Ritual: Same toss height, same tempo, same breath every time.
  • Balance: Shift weight forward smoothly — no rush, no lean.
  • Contact: Full extension, loose wrist, brush the ball, finish tall.

Consistency builds confidence — repetition builds rhythm.

Serve Types & Uses

Choose the right serve for the moment. Placement and purpose matter more than speed.

  • Flat Serve: Speed + accuracy to corners. Use at 30–0 or 40–15.
  • Slice Serve: Curves away from opponent; great on deuce side.
  • Kick Serve: High bounce and safety margin; ideal for second serves.
  • Body Serve: Jam the returner; great for pressure points.

You don’t need all four — master two, place them anywhere.

Serve Targets

Think in lanes, not lines. Aim for big areas, not corners.

  • Deuce Side: Wide slice, body jam, T flat.
  • Ad Side: Body serve, T kicker, wide curve.

Use targets like a game plan — not a guessing game.

Serve +1 Connection

Plan your first two shots before you toss. Serve patterns build control and rhythm under pressure.

  • Wide Serve → Open Court Forehand: Pull them off-court and attack the space.
  • Body Serve → Deep Cross-Court: Jam, recover middle, and own the rally start.

See Tactics for +1 patterns.

Pressure Serving

Breathe. Reset. Focus on the next toss, not the last miss.

  • Ritual Under Pressure: Keep the same routine every point.
  • Body Serve Safety: Jam the returner instead of forcing aces.
  • Visual Cue: Pick a single dimple on the ball — see it through contact.

Drills & Practice

  • 10-Target Challenge: 10 serves, 3 corners + 1 body. Track hits.
  • Serve + Recover: Serve, move to recovery mark, simulate +1.
  • Pressure Ladder: 10 serves in; every miss adds one more attempt.

See Drills for full progressions that turn serving practice into confidence reps.

Routine & Reflection

  • ⬜ Same toss rhythm every time
  • ⬜ Balanced follow-through
  • ⬜ First-serve % improving weekly
  • ⬜ Adjusted targets when under pressure

Your serve is your heartbeat — keep it steady.
