Serve

Start every point on your terms. A dependable serve isn’t about raw power — it’s about a repeatable routine, a trustworthy toss, and clear targets. Build control first; pace comes later.


Serve Fundamentals

  • Routine and rhythm: Same breath, bounce, and look every time; call your target before you start.
  • Reliable toss: Toss you can trust: Smooth arm, palm up, release at eye height; rise first, swing second.
  • Solid contact point: High, clean contact: Full reach, relaxed arm, finish tall; let momentum carry you forward.

Consistency builds confidence — repetition builds rhythm.

Serve Types & Uses

Choose the right serve for the score and the opponent. Placement and purpose beat speed.

  • Flat: Simple, direct; use at 30–0 or 40–15 to press a lead.
  • Slice: Curves away; great deuce-side wide and ad-side body.
  • Kick: Higher bounce and big margin; default second serve.
  • Body: Jam the returner; perfect for pressure points and deuce → backhand.

You don’t need all four — master two, place them anywhere.

Serve Targets

Think in lanes, not lines. Aim big and play the percentages.

  • Deuce: Wide slice, body jam, T flat.
  • Ad: Body serve, T kick, wide curve.
    • Cue: Start center and “miss” to the edges — not the other way around.

Use targets like a game plan — not a guessing game.

Serve +1 Connection

Your serve sets up your first groundstroke. Decide it before you toss.

  • Duece: Wide slice → attack open court; T flat → backhand corner.
  • Ad: Kick T → forehand inside-out; body serve → step in through middle.
  • Default plan: Big margin to the body, then rally ball deep middle.

See Tactics for +1 patterns.

Pressure Serving

Breathe. Reset. Focus on the next toss, not the last miss.

  • Ritual Under Pressure: Keep the same routine every point.
  • Body Serve Safety: Jam the returner instead of forcing aces.
  • Visual Cue: Pick a single dimple on the ball — see it through contact.

Quick Fixes

Small, repeatable adjustments keep your serve steady. Start with these before changing grip or motion.

  • Long misses: Toss too far forward or late contact → “Toss a fist back; reach first.”
  • Into the net: Contact too low → “Hit up to a high finish.”
  • Wild toss: Speeding the raise → “Elevator, not rocket.” Use the net post to guide your toss.

Drills & Practice

  • 10-Target Challenge: 10 serves, 3 corners + 1 body. Track hits.
  • Serve + Recover: Serve, move to recovery mark, simulate +1.
  • Pressure Ladder: 10 serves in; every miss adds one more attempt.

Routine & Reflection

  • ⬜ Same toss rhythm every time
  • ⬜ Balanced follow-through
  • ⬜ First-serve % improving weekly
  • ⬜ Adjusted targets when under pressure

Your serve is your heartbeat — keep it steady.
