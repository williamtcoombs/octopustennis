Serve

Your serve is your heartbeat — keep it steady. We build consistency first, then add pace and precision. Own a routine, trust your toss, and choose big targets you can hit under pressure.

The point begins before the ball leaves your hand: breathe, see the target, feel the rhythm, commit.

Coach’s Tip: Say a quiet cue before every serve: “Breathe → Target → Toss → Up.” Keep that script no matter the score.

Serve Fundamentals

We’re not chasing aces; we’re building a serve that shows up in tight moments.

  • Routine & rhythm: Same breath, bounce, and look every time. Call your target before you start.
  • Reliable toss: Relaxed arm; release around eye height; let it rise before you move. “Elevator, not rocket.”
  • Solid contact point: High and clean. Reach, extend, finish tall; let momentum carry you forward.

Consistency creates confidence; repetition builds rhythm.

Goal: 8/10 first serves in when aiming big targets; 9/10 second serves with kick or body.

Serve Types & Uses

Choose the right serve for the point. Placement and purpose beat speed.

  • Flat: Direct and simple — use at 30–0 or 40–15 to press a lead.
  • Slice: Curves away — great deuce-wide and ad-body to jam returns.
  • Kick: Height and margin — your default second serve.
  • Body: Jam the returner — high percentage on pressure points.

You don’t need all four — master two and place them anywhere.

Coach’s Tip: Build a two-serve identity (e.g., ad-side kick T, deuce-side slice wide) and own it.

For point construction to match these choices, see Strategy and Tactics.

Serve Targets

Think in lanes, not lines. Aim big and play the percentages.

Use broad zones — wide, body, or T — instead of razor-thin corners. Margin survives nerves.
  • Deuce: Wide slice, body jam, T flat.
  • Ad: Body serve, T kick, wide curve.
  • Cue: Start center and “miss” to the edges — not the other way around.

Goal: 6/10 first serves landing past the service line to your chosen lane, both sides.

Serve +1 Connection

Decide your +1 before the toss. Your first groundstroke is part of the serve.

  • Deuce: Slice wide → attack the open court; T flat → play to backhand corner.
  • Ad: Kick T → forehand inside-out; body serve → step through middle and take time.
  • Default plan: Big margin to the body, then a rally ball deep middle.

Coach’s Tip: Land balanced and ready — don’t watch the serve, watch your opponent’s contact.

See Tactics for +1 patterns.

Pressure Serving

Breathe, reset, simplify. The scoreboard doesn’t swing the racquet — your routine does.

  • Ritual Under Pressure: Exact same routine every point, especially on break points.
  • Body Serve Safety: Jam the returner — highest make-rate when tight.
  • Visual Cue: Pick one dimple on the ball; see it from toss to strings.

Reset Cue: Exhale as the toss rises → say “Up” at contact → hold your finish tall for one count.

Quick Fixes

Start here before changing grip or motion. Small, repeatable adjustments stabilize performance.

  • Long misses: Toss too far forward / late contact → “Toss a fist back; reach first.”
  • Into the net: Contact too low → “Hit up to a high finish.”
  • Wild toss: Raising too fast → “Elevator, not rocket.” Use the net post as your vertical guide.

Common Serve Errors

  • Rushing the toss (ball drifts) → slow your lift; release earlier.
  • Arm-only swing (no legs) → load through the hips and push the ground.
  • Dropping the head at contact → eyes up through the finish.
  • No plan for +1 ball → decide before you toss.

Fix: Film 10 serves from the side. Grade yourself on toss height, extension, and balance on landing.

Routine & Reflection

  • ⬜ Same toss rhythm every time
  • ⬜ Balanced follow-through (finish tall, recover ready)
  • ⬜ First-serve % improving weekly
  • ⬜ Adjusted targets when under pressure

Weekly Challenge: 75% first-serve make rate to body/T lanes in practice sets; second-serve double faults ≤ 1 per set.
