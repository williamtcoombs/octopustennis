Serve

Your serve is our heartbeat — steady, repeatable, and competitive. We build it so it holds under score pressure. Start with consistency first, then add pace and precision. Own a routine, trust your toss, and pick big targets you can hit under pressure.

The point begins before the ball leaves your hand: breathe, see the lane, feel the rhythm, commit fully.

Coach’s Cue: “Breathe → See → Commit → Feet set → Target → Toss → Up.” Keep that script no matter the score.

Serve Fundamentals

Master the ingredients you can control every single rep.

  • Routine & rhythm: Same breath, bounce, and look every time. Call your lane before you start.
  • Reliable toss: Relaxed arm; release at eye height; let it rise before you move. “Elevator, not rocket.”
  • Solid contact point: High and clean. Reach, extend, finish tall; let momentum carry you forward.

Consistency creates confidence; repetition builds rhythm.

Team Standard: 7/10 first serves to big lanes; 9/10 second serves with shape (kick or body).

Serve Types & Uses

Choose the right serve for the situation. Placement and purpose beat raw speed.

  • Flat: When you’re ahead in the game. T and into the body.
  • Slice: Deuce wide and Ad body. Pulls the returner off the court.
  • Kick: Ad side up the backhand or into the body for height and safety.

Simple Game Plans

Deuce side: Slice wide to pull the returner off court; body serve to jam; flat up the T when ahead in the game.

Ad side: Kick up the backhand for height; body into the hips; flat T to surprise on 30–love or game point.

Rule of three: Start each service game with three high-percentage patterns you trust. Win the first point, then expand.

Serve Targets

Own two lanes on each side: wide and T. Body serve is your third option under pressure.

  • Deuce: Wide slice, T flat.
  • Ad: Kick up the backhand, T flat.
  • Body: Jam the returner to limit swing.

Targeting & Margin

  • Net clearance: Aim 1–2 feet above the tape on firsts, 2–3 on seconds.
  • Depth: Land deep half of the service box; miss long rather than in the middle short.
  • A/B drill: Hit 10 serves “A” (wide) then 10 “B” (T). Goal: 14/20 total, with at least 6 to each target.

Measurable Checkpoints

  • First-serve reliability: 7/10 in your chosen target (no lets). If you miss two in a row, downshift pace, keep height and spin.
  • Second-serve trust: 8/10 clears the tape by ~2–3 ball heights with kick/slice; land deep half of the box.
  • Toss consistency: Drop test: 8/10 tosses would land within a small circle just inside your lead foot.
  • Contact: Hit at full reach; freeze the finish for one count to verify balance.
  • Recovery: Land inside the court, replace the back foot, and split as your opponent contacts the return.

Serve +1 Connection

Plan your first groundstroke based on serve choice.

  • Deuce wide slice → open court forehand.
  • Ad kick → step in on the short backhand.
  • Body → be ready to volley or take early on the rise.

Pressure Serving

Under score stress, simplify. Bigger margin, more spin, same routine.

  • Pick the body or kick lane and commit.
  • Same toss height — don’t shorten your motion.
  • See the finish and hold it for one count.

Pressure Routine

  1. Breathe: One slow inhale/exhale before the toss; unlock the shoulders.
  2. Commit: Name the target in your head (e.g., “Ad kick backhand”).
  3. Same tempo: Same toss height and rhythm as practice; don’t rush the drop.
  4. Finish tall: Hold the balanced finish one count to confirm posture.

Quick Fixes

  • Missing long: Raise net clearance; aim deeper kick/body rather than flat.
  • Missing in the net: Higher toss start; see the ball rise before you move.
  • Rushing: One extra breath before the toss; long exhale on contact.

Common Serve Errors

  • Toss drift: Comes from speed; soften the hand and lift, don’t throw.
  • Locked arm: Keep the shoulder loose; let the racquet fall naturally.
  • No finish: Hold the pose for one count to verify balance.

Routine & Reflection

Use the same routine whether you’re up or down. After practice or a match, reflect on one win and one tweak.

Quick Serve Log

After practice or match, jot two notes:

  • What worked: (e.g., “Deuce slice wide opened the court”)
  • Next tweak: (e.g., “Higher net clearance on second serve”)
