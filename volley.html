Command the net with quick reflexes, soft hands, and smart positioning.

Volley

Own the air game. Calm hands, quick feet, clear targets — finish points with composure.

Volley Fundamentals

  • Split & Stick: Split step as the opponent hits; short punch, not a swing.
  • Racquet Quiet: Firm wrist, leading edge slightly open; contact in front.
  • Feet First: Step through the ball; body supplies pace more than the arm.

Small movement, big control. Guide the ball — don’t wind up.

Court Position & Targets

  • Positions: Service line (transition), mid-net (standard), close-in (put-away).
  • Primary Targets: Deep middle to shrink angles; body to jam; short angle to pull wide.
  • Split Timing: Land your split as the opponent makes contact — then read and step.

Volley Types

Standard Volley

I was playing doubles with a former tennis coach as my partner. He told me, "You do a good job of keeping your racquet out in front of you." I'm not sure if it was true at the time, but it has been since.



Transition Volley

Taken near the service line while moving in. Prioritize height + depth to buy time; recover forward to mid-net.

Drop Volley

Soften the grip at contact; short follow-through. Use when opponent is deep or off balance. Place just over the net with angle.

Lob Volley

Open face and lift with legs. Great against aggressive net rushers or when jammed. Aim high to backhand side.

Half Volley

Low, short hop just after the bounce. Keep racquet quiet, accept the ball, and guide deep middle to reset.

Drive Volley

Taken high and rising. Compact swing, contact well in front, drive toward open court. Use when set and balanced.

Put-Away Volley

I was at a tennis camp once, playing with the head pro as my partner. I got an easy ball at the net which I returned weakly and we lost the point. He said, "You have to put that away." I thought to myself, "I don't know how."



Patterns at Net

  • Deep Middle → Angle: First volley deep middle; second creates the angle.
  • Body Jam → Open Court: Jam to chest, then finish to space.
  • Drop → Lob: If they sprint forward, show a soft drop then lift the next ball over.

Two-ball plans keep you composed under pressure.

Drills

  • Service-Line Wall: 50 quick punch volleys, focus on quiet hands.
  • Deep-Middle First: Coach feeds; first volley deep middle, second to angle.
  • Half-Volley Reset: Low feeds at service line, guide to deep middle.
  • Drop & Lob Series: Alternate soft drop with controlled lob volley.

Volley Checklist

  • ⬜ Split timed with opponent contact
  • ⬜ Contact out in front, quiet wrist
  • ⬜ First volley deep middle
  • ⬜ Finish with feet toward the target
