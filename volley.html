Volley Fundamentals

A great volley starts with split timing, compact preparation, and body stability through contact.

  • Split Step: Time your split as your opponent hits.
  • Compact Swing: No backswing — punch forward with controlled firmness.
  • Balance: Keep knees soft and chest facing target.

Coach’s Cue: “Split → Read → Step.”

Court Position & Targets

Smart positioning turns defense into offense. Own the middle first, then close space with every volley.

  • First volley: aim deep middle 8/10 times.
  • Close diagonally after contact; cover the line with your partner.
  • Finish the point with clear angles only when balanced.

Volley Types

  • Standard Volley: Controlled punch through the middle.
  • Approach Volley: Deep and firm to the corner, follow in behind.
  • Drop Volley: Feathered, soft hands, ball dies short.
  • Lob Volley: Defensive lift over aggressive net player.
  • Half Volley: Pick-up off the bounce, steady and compact.
  • Drive Volley: Aggressive put-away in mid-air.

Patterns at Net

Anticipation and positioning make your volley game unstoppable.

  • Poach Pattern: Read serve direction, move early across middle.
  • Cover + Finish: First volley deep middle, second volley angled off.
  • Reset + Recover: When stretched, volley high middle, recover stance.

Drills

  • Rapid Fire (5 mins): 10 volleys in a row from service line, reset feet each rep.
  • Triangle Net Drill: Alternate middle-left-right volleys, 3 cycles.
  • Approach + Finish: Approach volley, close, put-away — 5 rounds each side.

Goal: Win 70% of net exchanges in practice sets.

Volley Checklist

  • ⬜ Timed split step
  • ⬜ Compact swing, no backswing
  • ⬜ Chest to target through contact
  • ⬜ Quick recovery ready for next ball
