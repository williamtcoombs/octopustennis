Own the air game with quiet hands and decisive feet. Split on time, punch to big targets, and recover forward.

Coach’s Cue: “Split → Read → Step.” Hands guide, feet deliver.

Volley Fundamentals

  • Split & Stick: Land the split as they hit; short punch, not a swing.
  • Racquet Quiet: Firm wrist, slightly open face; meet the ball out front.
  • Feet First: Step through toward target — body supplies most of the pace.

Small move, big control. Guide, don’t wind up.

Team Standard: 8/10 first volleys placed deep middle from the service line.

Court Position & Targets

  • Positions: Service line (transition), mid‑net (standard), close‑in (put‑away).
  • Primary Targets: Deep middle to shrink angles; body to jam; short angle to pull wide.
  • Split Timing: Land split on opponent contact, then read and step.

Cue: “Middle first.” Earn the sharp angle on ball two.

Volley Types

Standard Volley

Neutral ball, waist height. Racquet out front, quiet wrist, short punch to a big target (deep middle or body).

Goal: Make 9/10 standard volleys to deep middle; hold finish for one count.



Approach Volley

Taken near the service line while moving in. Prioritize height + depth to buy time, then recover forward to mid‑net.

Goal: 8/10 land past service line with 3+ feet net clearance; recover two steps forward.


Drop Volley

Soften the grip at contact; short follow‑through. Use when the opponent is deep or off balance. Place just over the net with angle.

Goal: Die the ball inside the service box with two bounces before the baseline.


Lob Volley

Open the face and lift with the legs. Great against aggressive net rushers or when jammed. Aim high to the backhand side.

Goal: Clear opponent by 8–10 feet; land deep and force a backpedal.


Half Volley

Low, short hop just after the bounce. Keep the racquet quiet, accept the ball, and guide deep middle to reset.

Goal: 7/10 guided to deep middle without a swing — just a firm block.


Drive Volley

Taken high and rising. Compact swing, contact well in front, drive toward open court. Use when set and balanced.

Goal: 8/10 driven to open court; finish chest to target.


Put‑Away Volley

High sitter or slow floater. Step through, contact in front, finish through the court to space — no second chances at the net.

Goal: Finish 9/10 within two swings; choose space, not the line judge.



Patterns at Net

  • Deep Middle → Angle: First volley deep middle; second creates the angle.
  • Body Jam → Open Court: Jam to chest, then finish to space.
  • Drop → Lob: If they sprint forward, show a soft drop then lift the next ball over.

Coach’s Tip: Call the target before contact (middle, body, or angle). Decisiveness is half the volley.

Two‑ball plans keep you composed under pressure.

Drills

  • Service‑Line Wall: 50 quick punch volleys; quiet hands, racquet out front.
  • Deep‑Middle First: Coach feeds; first volley deep middle, second to angle.
  • Half‑Volley Reset: Low feeds at service line; guide to deep middle, hold your finish.
  • Drop & Lob Series: Alternate soft drop with controlled lob volley.

Goal: Win 7/10 first‑volley exchanges in practice points by choosing deep middle first.

Volley Checklist

  • ⬜ Split timed with opponent contact
  • ⬜ Contact out in front, quiet wrist
  • ⬜ First volley deep middle
  • ⬜ Finish with feet toward the target

Weekly Challenge: In doubles drills, convert 70% of net‑position points when you touch the first volley.
