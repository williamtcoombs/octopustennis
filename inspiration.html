Inspiration

Keep growing, stay curious, and lift others as you climb — champions build champions.

Inspiration

Champions are built on effort, integrity, and teamwork. The lessons we learn on court shape who we become off it.

Coach’s Tip: Before practice, say three words to yourself: Steady. Brave. Grateful. Carry them through your day.

Our Core Values

  • Effort: Give your best every point, every practice, every day.
  • Integrity: Play fair, call lines honestly, and respect the game.
  • Teamwork: Lift each other—energy travels.

On the fence: Encourage with cues, not critique: “Height.” “Middle.” “Feet.”

Team Creed

“We rise together — one team, one energy.”
“We bring effort when we’re tired and encouragement when others are.”
“We lift our teammates, respect our opponents, and protect our culture.”
“We finish with gratitude — for the game, the growth, and each other.”

Say one creed line as a team huddle before every match.

Leadership & Growth

Leadership starts with consistency—showing up, staying kind, and setting the tone. Every player can lead through effort and example.

  • Model body language you’d want from a partner.
  • Own mistakes quickly—then move on quickly.
  • Celebrate teammates’ hustle plays, not just winners.

Gratitude & Legacy

We play for more than a score: the teammates beside us, the younger players watching, and the people who make our season possible.

  • Thank a parent, teacher, or volunteer after home matches.
  • Leave courts cleaner than we found them.
  • Teach one skill to a younger player this month.

Reflection Prompts

Take one minute before leaving practice—or this page—to reflect:

  • What moment made you proud today?
  • Who did you encourage and how?
  • Where did you choose courage over comfort?

Inspiration Checklist

  • ⬜ Encouraged a teammate with a clear cue
  • ⬜ Learned from one challenge without complaint
  • ⬜ Led with integrity (calls, conduct, respect)
  • ⬜ Expressed gratitude to someone specific

Weekly Challenge: Write three short gratitude notes (one to a teammate, one to a teacher, one to family). Share one takeaway with the team.

Coach’s Corner

Inspiration

Programme culture and personal ownership. Inspiration is not hype — it’s the daily habits and choices that shape who we become together. Use this page to keep effort, integrity, teamwork, and composure visible in how you run practices, give feedback, and respond to tough moments.

What to Reinforce

  • Inspiration is habits plus meaning. Connect every story, quote, or clip back to a specific practice behavior (how we warm up, how we talk to partners, how we compete late in practice).
  • Leadership can be quiet. Celebrate players who lead with consistency, encouragement, and preparation — not just volume or big speeches.
  • Keep our pillars visible: effort, integrity, teamwork, composure. Use that language in feedback, not just on posters or slides.
  • Help each player own a personal “why” for playing tennis and revisit it at key moments in the season (tryouts, mid-season, playoffs, senior night).

Common Red Flags

  • Players chasing highlight-reel moments but checking out on basics: warm-up effort, footwork reps, routines between points.
  • Comparison mindset — talking about rankings, other teams, or “who’s better” more than talking about their own growth and controllables.
  • All-or-nothing self-talk after mistakes (“I always choke,” “I’m just bad at this”) instead of specific adjustments.
  • Team “values” only showing up in pre-season talks, not in how we respond to tough losses, bench roles, or limited playing time.

Key Cues & Corrections

  • “Play for the team in front of you, not the crowd behind you.”
  • “Small honest reps beat big talk.”
  • “Leaders go first in effort, last in excuses.”
  • When frustration rises, shift them to: “What’s one thing I can control this point?”

Observation Drill

Run a 10-minute “identity huddle” once a week. Each player writes one sentence: “This week I showed our team identity when I…” Then share around the circle. Listen for concrete behaviors (effort, support, resilience) rather than results. Target: everyone speaks once, and at least 8/10 examples describe actions, not scores.

Captain’s Checklist

Inspiration

Use this as your leadership script. Your role is to protect our culture: how we treat each other, how we respond to adversity, and how we leave the courts.

Before Practice / Match

  • Greet teammates by name as they arrive — set the tone with eye contact and energy.
  • Make sure music, warm-up, and huddles stay inclusive (no one on the outside of the circle).
  • Ask one teammate a simple check-in: “How are you doing today?”

During Practice

  • Notice who is quiet, frustrated, or alone — go to them first.
  • Cheer with specific cues (“Feet,” “Height,” “Middle”) instead of generic hype.
  • Model hustle between drills and to pick up balls; never walk when others are working.

During Matches

  • Protect the fence culture — no eye rolls, no blaming partners, no negative chatter.
  • Lead claps and specific encouragement after tough points, not just after winners.
  • Help younger players understand cheering etiquette and sportsmanship expectations.

After Practice / Match

  • Thank coaches, opponents, and anyone who helped run the day.
  • Check that courts, benches, and benches are clean — water bottles, towels, balls.
  • Ask one teammate, “What are you proud of from today?” and actually listen.

Off-Court & Legacy

  • Welcome new players by name; show them where gear goes and how we do things here.
  • Live the same values in hallways and classrooms that we talk about on court.
  • Help organize one simple give-back each season (younger-kids clinic, court clean-up, or thank-you notes).

Captain’s Cue: “Inspiration is how we treat people, especially on hard days. Champions build champions — starting with how we show up for each other.”
