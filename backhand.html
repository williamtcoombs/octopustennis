Backhand

Stay balanced, stay smooth. A solid backhand keeps the court — and your confidence — open.


Backhand Fundamentals

  • Set Early: Turn shoulders before the bounce — racquet back as the ball rises.
  • Contact in Front: Meet the ball slightly ahead of the body line.
  • Finish Fully: Relax shoulders; complete your swing every time.

A clean backhand is all about timing and trust.

One-Handed vs. Two-Handed

  • Two-Handed: Stronger stability, easier to control under pressure.
  • One-Handed: More reach, spin, and variation; requires earlier prep.

Different tools, same goal — balance and control.

Contact & Spin

  • Brush up and through for topspin control.
  • Stay sideways through contact to guide direction.
  • Lift with legs, not wrists — let the racquet flow.

Topspin adds safety — confidence adds speed.

Footwork & Positioning

  • Load on outside leg, step into contact.
  • Keep head still through contact.
  • Recover immediately back to center.

Hit, recover, and re-balance — that’s your rhythm anchor.

Variations & Tactics

  • Cross-Court Rally: Build rhythm and depth safely.
  • Down-the-Line Change-Up: Only when balanced.
  • Slice Backhand: Reset the point, add variety.
  • Drive Approach: Attack short balls deep middle or line.

Every variation earns you time, space, or opportunity.

Common Errors

  • Over-rotation or falling away on contact.
  • Late preparation.
  • Flicking wrist instead of driving through.

When in doubt, stay sideways and finish your swing.

Drills

  • Cross-Court Rally (10 mins): Keep 10 deep with shape.
  • Down-the-Line Challenge: Hit 3 line, recover middle.
  • Slice + Drive Combo: Alternate 2 slices, 2 drives for control.

See Drills for backhand-specific progressions.

Backhand Checklist

  • ⬜ Early shoulder turn before bounce
  • ⬜ Contact in front of body line
  • ⬜ Smooth finish, full extension
  • ⬜ Recovery inside the hash

Balanced, repeatable, and trusted — that’s your backhand.
