The Backhand

“You don’t rise to the occasion; you fall to the level of your training.” — Archilochus

Stroke Development Tiers

Control first, then pressure, then variety. Use this roadmap to prioritize training.

Tier 1: Fundamentals Tier 2: Offensive Development Tier 3: Advanced / Specialized
Tennis strokes organized by tier, purpose, and coaching notes.
Tier Stroke Purpose / Focus Coaching Notes
Tier 1 — Fundamentals Forehand Core rally weapon; most-used shot. Repeatable swing path, topspin control, contact timing.
Tier 1 — Fundamentals Backhand (two-handed) Balances baseline play and returns. Compact prep, body rotation, stable contact.
Tier 1 — Fundamentals Serve (flat) Starts every point. Consistent toss, contact height; add spin later.
Tier 1 — Fundamentals Return of Serve Neutralize opponent’s advantage. Short back-swing; block vs. pace, drive vs. slower serves.
Tier 1 — Fundamentals Slice (groundstroke) Defensive or rhythm-change tool. Brush under; keep ball low; great for low balls.
Tier 1 — Fundamentals Volley (FH/BH) Net finishing tool. Compact punch; firm wrist; out in front.
Tier 1 — Fundamentals Overhead Smash Finish high balls and lobs. Footwork first; early shoulder turn; reach high.
Tier 2 — Offensive Dev. Aggressive FH/BH Turn neutral into offense. Racquet-head speed; depth first, then angle/power.
Tier 2 — Offensive Dev. Slice / Chip Return Change pace; force awkward replies. Knife through; aim deep middle or at feet.
Tier 2 — Offensive Dev. Transition Volley / Approach Move forward and shorten points. Deep to corners; follow in; split-step timing.
Tier 2 — Offensive Dev. Drive Volley Attack floaters near service line. Groundstroke swing in air; contact in front.
Tier 2 — Offensive Dev. Drop Shot / Drop Volley Exploit deep positioning. Same swing start; soften grip; finish short.
Tier 2 — Offensive Dev. Lob (defensive — backspin) Reset under pressure. Lift through; height > speed; land deep middle.
Tier 3 — Advanced Kick Serve (topspin) Reliable second serve; high bounce. Upward brush; full follow-through; aim cross first.
Tier 3 — Advanced Slice Serve Pulls opponent wide; sets up next ball. Work both sides; vary body/T placement.
Tier 3 — Advanced Lob (offensive — topspin) Pass net-rusher; dip fast, bounce high. Heavy brush; give yourself net clearance.
Tier 3 — Advanced Lob Volley Surprise or defensive net tactic. Soft hands; prioritize height, then depth.
Tier 3 — Advanced Half Volley Bridge between defense and offense. Quiet hands; use body lift to control launch.
